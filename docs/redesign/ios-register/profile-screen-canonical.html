Same surface, different vocabulary

Profile is universal — hero, interest switcher, capability map, evidence drilldown, public credential, settings. What adapts per interest is the capability names. Sailing speaks wind-reading, starts, rules, tactical-decisions, mark-roundings; a nursing learner sees patient-assessment, documentation, clinical-reasoning, communication, handoffs. The dot ladder, the chips, the spacing — all bonded.

The credential is the evidence

No badges, no XP, no streaks. The capability rows are chips + dots + count and nothing else; tapping drills into the user's own quoted words from Plan, Do, and Reflect. The most a "level" earns is a small chip color shift and one more filled dot. The model is LinkedIn endorsements through deliberate practice, not gamified progression.

Privacy is opt-in, not opt-out

Profile is private by default. Even after the user flips it public, evidence quotes stay collapsed until they explicitly choose to show them. Per-interest visibility lets a sailor share their racing map without exposing their reading log. The settings sheet always lands with the safer state.
