BetterAt · Series Feature · Canonical — 2026-05-16

The Season wrapped around the practice — a time-bounded container for steps, with a jump-to picker for navigating inside it

Series is the frame; steps are what lives inside. Season for sailors, Term for nursing students, Workshop for drawing students, Block for fitness — same surface, per-interest vocabulary. The user belongs to exactly one Series at a time per interest, sees their progress through it on every surface, and can switch between past, current, and upcoming Series through one iOS-native action sheet.

The Frame 4 jump-to picker also retires the existing step picker — per Kevin's note today, the new name is Jump to, and the picker is contextualized to the Series the user is currently inside.

Jump-to picker

A near-full-height sheet over a dimmed step card. Header (Back · Jump to · X), Season subhead, progress bar, dense list of all 14 steps with number, title, phase dots; the current step is highlighted in iOS-blue. The picker doubles as the renamed step picker.

Series is the frame, not a filter

The user belongs to one Series at a time per interest. Past Series are readable but read-only; future Series can be pre-populated with planned steps. The Series chip on the zoomed-out view is the user's anchor — every step they see, by default, lives inside the current Season. Switching Series is an explicit action (Frame 2) that swaps the whole timeline context.

Gold trophy mark · time-bounded container

The 6 of 14 counter is the jump-to affordance

The existing step counter in the card header gets a subtle pill background and a chevron, signaling tap to jump. The picker that opens (Frame 4) is the renamed step picker — same dense list pattern as the zoomed-out view, but contextualized to the active Season and compressed to 14 rows instead of 147. Current step is highlighted with the same iOS-blue treatment used everywhere else in the register.

iOS blue · 6 of 14 → Jump-to picker

Phase dots are the shared dialect

The three-phase status (Plan · Do · Reflect) appears in three places now: the zoomed-out list, the action sheet's secondary content, and the jump-to picker. Same glyph everywhere — filled done · half in progress · empty not started. The user learns it once.
