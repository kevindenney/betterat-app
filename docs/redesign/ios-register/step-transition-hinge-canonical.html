What this iOS register commits to

The hinge becomes a filmstrip on iOS.

On desktop, the hinge is one centered editorial column you encounter mid-swipe between Race 3 Debrief and Race 4 Prep — the italic-serif anchor "Three days between" over a single synthesis paragraph.

On iOS that single column would feel too quiet on a phone screen: the swipe gesture already lives in horizontal motion, so the hinge here borrows that grammar. Each day in the interval is its own tile, horizontally scrollable. The header carries the synthesis; the tiles carry the days.

The principle preserved across the register shift: the time between named moments is named, and what filled it is shown. The form rotates, the principle holds.

Day-tiles are deliberately lower visual weight than step cards.

tile · #FFFFFF ground · #F2F2F7

The hinge is a connective surface, not a destination, and the tiles say so visually:

  • No card shadowstep-card components elsewhere in the iOS register carry a 1px stroke-shadow. Day tiles do not. Their weight is the white-on-gray-6 contrast alone.
  • Title weight 400step-card titles are 17px semibold (600). Day-tile bodies are 17px regular. One weight down.
  • Eyebrow does the title’s workWEDNESDAY · MARCH 18 carries the identity of the tile. The body is the moment, not a heading.
  • Hairline provenance, not a signal rowthe provenance sits in 12px tertiary, the smallest type on the surface. It tells you where the moment came from and when, and it does not compete.

If the hinge ever felt as substantial as a step, the system would be telling Felix this is somewhere he should arrive. It isn't. Lower weight is the truth-telling.

Three kinds of day, named at the top of each tile.

Across the three-day interval, Felix's activity falls into a small grammar — each tile labels itself with what kind of day it was:

  • A flagged momentsomething Felix returned to, marked, replayed. Wednesday: re-watching Sam’s coaching note.
  • A reflectionsomething he wrote about, in his own voice, after the fact. Thursday: the right side winning for the wrong reason.
  • A captured notesomething short he wrote down for next time, often becoming a rule. Friday: the rule for Race 4.

The kind label is provenance you can scan — you know what kind of moment a tile is before you read it. The same three kinds appear on other hinges with different content; an empty day is allowed to read as "no entry" rather than be padded into a fake moment.

Bookends sit beneath the filmstrip.

The two named moments this hinge connects — Race 3 Debrief and Race 4 Prep — appear as a small grouped list under the filmstrip. Same component grammar as iOS settings rows.

These are not the primary affordance. They’re here because the hinge’s job is to make the connection visible; showing what it connects is part of that job. Tap-targets, not visual emphasis. The filmstrip above does the editorial work.

What this surface deliberately doesn’t do

  • No standalone synthesis paragraphthe desktop hinge has one editorial paragraph. On iOS, the title “Three days at the edge” and the three named days are the synthesis. Adding a paragraph would push toward the desktop component instead of adapting.
  • No coral dot, anywherethe Discovery accent is reserved for Discover surfaces only. The hinge sits on the Race tab.
  • No reaction or comment chrome on tilesmoments are not posts. Tiles tap through to their source surface and nothing else.
  • No timeline scrubberthe desktop scrub control is review-pane scaffolding for the reviewer. The iOS register doesn’t need it — the user’s swipe through the Race timeline is the scrubber.
  • No segmented controlno “All / Flagged / Reflections / Notes”. The interval is three days; filtering it is overbuilt. If the interval ever grew past a week, the question reopens.

iOS register grammar preserved

  • System gray 6 groundidentical to Paths-for-you, Race Prep, Get Inspired, On the Water iOS.
  • SF Pro Text / SF Pro Displaysystem fonts only on the device surface.
  • 34px regular large titlesame lighter-than-declarative weight used on Paths-for-you. The hinge isn’t shouting.
  • 11px semibold caps eyebrowsame component used to label sections across iOS, redeployed here once per tile as the day name.
  • Floating four-tab navsame component, Race tab active in iOS blue.
  • Grouped-list bookendsstandard iOS settings-row grammar, 16px radius white container, 0.5px separators.

Behaviour spec for engineering

  • Entryswipe between adjacent races in the Race timeline. Not invokable from anywhere else — no tab, no URL, no entry affordance from the timeline overview.
  • Tile countone per day in the interval. Empty days are allowed (eyebrow + “no entry”). Caps at ~7 visible tiles; longer intervals show a head and tail with a count in the middle.
  • Tile tapopens the source surface for that moment (reflection in Concept · Trust the shift, or the captured note in Race 4 Prep). The hinge is not where the moment lives, just where it’s indexed.
  • Phrasingvaries with the gap. The night between · The morning between · Three days at the edge · A week between · Two months between. The eyebrow names the interval in sailor’s language, not a metric.
