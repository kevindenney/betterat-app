What

Mental routine before each start.

How

  1. Six slow breaths on the rail at the 4-minute gun, eyes closed.
  2. Name the day's wind: shifty / steady / dying — out loud, to the crew.
  3. Pick one cue word for the first beat (e.g. "patient"). Repeat it on the line.

Why

Helps me settle into a calm, decisive headspace when the start gets noisy.

Other person's step

A familiar Plan layout — but on warm cream stock, with a small attribution strip and a lock pill marking it as someone else's. The whole screen funnels to one button: Add this to my timeline.

The social layer is subordinated, by design

The user's own practice is the foreground. The social layer is a quieter ground beneath it: warmer tone, smaller type (13.5pt vs 15pt), no phase-status dots, no NOW divider, no FAB interaction. Cards have rounded edges and breathing room rather than the flush, dense row-rhythm of the user's timeline. Scrolling past the user's own history is the only way to find it — and one tap on Hide collapses the whole zone if it ever starts to feel noisy.

Following band — muted warm ground

Three sub-sections answer three different social questions

People you follow shows individual recent activity — five most-recent step thumbnails per person on a horizontal rail. Blueprints you follow shows the structure of a plan with your own progress pinned onto it as a blue chip. Peers on the same blueprint compresses many followed practitioners' positions into three lines, with a small bar showing where each is relative to where you are. One blue tick-mark in every bar always means "you."

Tap a step → familiar shape, foreign content, one obvious next move

Frame 3 reuses the Plan-tab structure of the user's own steps so it reads instantly. But it's on a different stock — warm cream, not iOS cool gray — and gains an attribution strip and a small lock pill marking the privacy boundary. Body text loses one shade of weight. Everything funnels toward the one full-width blue button: Add this to my timeline. Save and Share are quiet text actions below; there is no edit, because this isn't yours yet.
