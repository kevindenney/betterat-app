Frame 3 · Public profiles & footer

Bottom of scroll · graduate records, then three doors out

The graduate grid is the page’s most opinionated section. Each card carries a verified chip — the same dark-ink chip as the hero — and three top capabilities rendered as 5-dot progress rows in tenant ink, so “strong in” reads as evidence-backed and not a self-claim. Anonymous graduates get the same card shape with a dashed border and a question-mark disc; the grammar is identical, the data is the same, only attribution is withheld. The footer puts three CTAs side-by-side — Apply takes the primary block (dark tenant ink, white button), Learn more and Newsroom are equal-weight neighbors.

Mobile-responsive degradation exists — the AACN grid stacks to 2 cols then 1, the blueprint strip becomes a horizontal swipe, the graduate grid stacks to a single column, and the footer CTAs become a stack — but the canonical view is desktop. The public org page is the one BetterAt surface that needs to look right when shared as a link, embedded in an email signature, or projected in a conference room.

Why the public page inherits BetterAt’s chrome, not the school’s

BetterAt is the medium, the institution is the content. Same black BetterAt mark, same minimal public-nav, same warm institutional cream as the org-admin hero. The tenant gets the shield, the name, and the color of one accent line; everything else is platform.

This is what keeps the product feeling like one product across betterat.app/jhu, betterat.app/rhkyc, and betterat.app/pradan. A visitor browsing two institutions in two tabs should never feel like they switched products.

The verified chip, used three times

Identical mark in three places: in the hero next to the school name, on every graduate card, and in the site footer. The signal is the same — this profile is platform-verified — so the visual treatment doesn’t modulate.

Anonymous graduates keep their verified chip even though their name is withheld. The verification is of the evidence, not the identity. That distinction matters more on this page than anywhere else in the product.

What the live numbers are doing

The three big stats under The Program in Practice are not marketing claims. They’re cohort metrics drawn from the same source the Org Admin Dashboard reads from — refreshed in the last quarter hour, same as the admin’s “Term in session, Wk 11 of 16” pill.

Numbers earn the visual weight. We render them in 64-px serif lining figures and let them sit between hairlines — the same proportion the iOS register uses for the trophy stat on Race Log.

What got skipped

The Apply flow itself lives off-platform; the CTA opens westbrook.edu in a new tab. The Newsroom is one click out of this page — its content lives at a separate URL not covered here.

The mobile view exists in the responsive companion canonical. The desktop view is the canonical because this is the page most likely to be shared as a link, included in a press kit, or projected in an admissions session.
