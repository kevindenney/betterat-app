The Playbook tab: where you develop your understanding of the field — concepts as the unit, not steps

Playbook is one of two long-arc surfaces in the product. Practice is where you take action; Playbook is where you develop your understanding. The unit is the concept. You come across a half-thought reading an article, hear someone say something on a late-night nursing shift, write a line in Reflect that feels bigger than the step — and you drop it into Playbook. Over time, that raw insight gets refined: it acquires a name, gets linked to active steps for testing, and eventually settles as part of your foundations. Three decisions land here (D18–D20), plus the four-tab spine made explicit and the Concept detail surface rendered in the same iOS register the rest of the product uses.

SpineThe four-tab spine, made explicit

One verb per tab. One unit per tab. One thing that accumulates. Read left to right — these aren't a navigation; they're the four layers of becoming. Practice is the action layer, Playbook the understanding layer, Discover the social-input layer, Profile the public layer where the loop becomes shareable. Two long-arc threads run beneath: capability evidence (Practice → Profile) and concept refinement (Playbook → Profile).

LifecycleConcept lifecycle · four stages, four colors, one trajectory

A concept enters as a seed — half-thought, no name yet. It moves to forming when the user returns to it (revisits, refines the language, adds a related quote). It graduates to testing the moment it's linked to an active step in Practice — that's the empirical moment, where understanding gets tried against doing. It settles when the user explicitly promotes it (or when the system detects it's been stably referenced across multiple settled steps). The state pill color tracks the stage.

LandingPlaybook landing · three zones by lifecycle stage

The tab opens to a single scrollable surface organized top-down by concept maturity. Hero strip at the top names the playbook's totals. Zone 1 (Recent insights) shows raw captures awaiting refinement — dashed borders signal "not yet a concept." Zone 2 (Concepts in development) shows what you're actively working on — purple-or-amber state pills, italic-serif title in your own voice. Zone 3 (Settled foundations) is the closed library — your accumulated understanding, alphabetical or chronological. Tap any row in any zone to push into that concept's detail surface.

ThreadHow a concept connects to a step · the empirical loop

Concepts don't live in isolation in Playbook — they get tested in Practice. The threading runs in both directions: a concept proposes itself onto the Plan tab (as a "what you're working with" chip), gets exercised in Do (captures may evidence the concept), and confirms in Reflect (user marks "this step deepened my understanding of X"). Reflect's confirmation flows back to Playbook as a new trail quote on the concept.

The empirical loop: a concept that survives this round-trip three or four times graduates to Settled. A concept that doesn't keep showing up in captures stays in Forming — the system's polite way of saying "this might not be a real concept yet."

1 · Concepts are foundations, not notes.

A note is something you write down so you don't forget. A concept is something you've come to understand. The Playbook tab is for the second kind. The dashed-border insight cards on Zone 1 are the holding pen for raw material; refinement (a name, a synthesis, a link to a step) is what promotes them. "Note" is not a status in this system. "Concept" is.

seed forming testing settled

2 · Italic serif is concept voice.

Every concept title across the product is italic serif in the user's own quoted phrase. Same component as Trophy's titles, Reflect's moments-returned-to, and the synthesis blocks. The italic-serif-with-provenance grammar is the visual signal that this is a phrase you wrote, not a system label. Sans titles ("Race 4 in 18–22 knots") describe events; italic-serif titles ("Trust the shift, not just the side") describe understanding.

events sans · concepts italic serif

3 · The empirical loop is the heart of the product.

Concepts that don't survive contact with practice stay in Forming. Concepts that do — that keep showing up in captures and getting confirmed in reflections — graduate. This is the product's distinctive claim: understanding is earned by testing, not by writing. The lifecycle states aren't decorative; they enforce the discipline.

tested in 3+ steps to graduate

4 · Knowledge enters from many places.

The universal + sheet (D6) has a Drop a concept entry-point. Long-press a capture in Do tab to mark-as-concept-seed. Long-press a paragraph in Reflect for the same. Long-press someone else's step on their timeline to save the idea behind it. Four entry points, one inbox — Zone 1 on the Playbook landing. The user never has to think "where does this thought go?" — it goes to Playbook, and they refine it later.

+ capture reflection others' steps

5 · The footer CTA tracks lifecycle.

A Forming concept's CTA is Link to a step — pushing the user to test it. A Testing concept's CTA is Promote to settled — but only when it has 3+ evidence steps; otherwise the button is disabled with hint copy. A Settled concept has no footer CTA — it's a destination, not a doorway. Same pattern as Trophy: when the artifact is complete, the chrome quiets.

link to a step promote no CTA

6 · Playbook + Practice = two parallel arcs feeding Profile.

Capabilities accumulate from Practice (Plan tag → Do evidence → Reflect confirm). Concepts accumulate from Playbook (Insight → Forming → Testing → Settled). Both feed Profile — capabilities populate the capability map, concepts populate the foundations list. Both feed Profile of Becoming as long-arc evidence. Two arcs, one becoming.
