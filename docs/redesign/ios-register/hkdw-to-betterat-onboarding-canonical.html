From the Hong Kong Dragon Worlds app to the first action inside BetterAt — a four-phase journey

A racing sailor preparing for the Worlds in November 2026 lands on BetterAt through their event app. Phase 1: inside HKDW, they see an offer for a complementary 3-month subscription to Kevin Denney's Prepare for the Hong Kong Dragon Worlds blueprint. Phase 2: a thin handoff moment as the deep link opens BetterAt with a redeem token in tow. Phase 3: a welcoming surface in BetterAt — the byline is Kevin's, the blueprint preview is honest about what 12 weeks looks like, the fleet count tells them they're joining a group. Phase 4: their first action — Practice tab pre-populated with Step 1, AI Coach quiet at the top, suggestions row showing fleet peers who already started, WITH-row naming the 63-sailor Worlds Fleet they just joined. The whole journey takes under a minute and the user never has to make an account decision they didn't sign up for.

The architectural rule: any surface that reads works in web — Plan, Reflect, Playbook, Discover, Profile, hinges, fleet view. Any surface that captures in real time is where the native app earns its install. The install never gates an offer; it deepens an experience already in motion.

1 · The bridge respects both apps.

HKDW's "About BetterAt" tab renders in HKDW's chrome (navy event-app palette, their tab vocabulary, their banner). The BetterAt offer is one white card with a single bridging visual hint — the purple sparkle eyebrow and italic-serif title — that gives the user a preview of where they're going without colonizing the host app's design. Two apps, one handoff, neither pretending to be the other.

HKDW chrome BetterAt offer card

2 · Kevin is the byline, not BetterAt.

The user is being welcomed by a person they trust — Kevin Denney, the Worlds-qualified Dragon helm whose blueprint they're being offered. The platform comes second. This is true on the offer card in HKDW (Kevin's avatar is the first thing), in the handoff caption ("Kevin Denney is welcoming you"), and in the redeem landing (his avatar at 60 px, his name in italic). The brand transfer happens through the author, not the product.

author byline · 4 places · same name

3 · No account form before redeem.

The deep link carries a single-use redeem token. The user accepts the offer first, lands in a working app with their blueprint subscribed, and only sees an account-creation prompt later if they want to keep their data after the 3-month free window. The cost of trying is zero clicks of forms. Account capture happens after the user has experienced the product, not as the gate to experiencing it.

token-based · 3-month grace period

4 · The fleet badge is social proof, not pressure.

"63 Worlds sailors already started" is honest — the count matches the actual number of HKDW sailors who redeemed. The avatar stack on the redeem landing shows real people the user could click through to see (privacy permitting). On the first-action surface, the WITH-row chip carries forward the same 63: "Worlds Fleet · 63 sailors." Same number, same fleet, the user reads continuity.

fleet count · 4 surfaces · same number

5 · The first action is small.

Step 1 is not the hardest step in the blueprint. It's a baseline-measurement step — sail for an hour, record target speeds on each point of sail. Concrete. Reachable in the next afternoon's practice. The user reads the title and knows what to do. The AI Coach helper is there if they want it, the suggestion row is there if they want to copy a peer, but the path is clear without either: tap WHAT, write a sentence, the CTA enables.

one tap · one sentence · CTA enables

6 · The journey is reversible.

Every step from the offer onward has a Not now. The HKDW tab has no auto-redeem. The redeem landing has a top-right Not now. The first-action toast can be dismissed. If the user accepts and then never returns, their blueprint subscription quietly expires after 3 months — no nags, no emails, no debt. The product has to earn the user's return, not capture them.

Not now × 3 places · expires silently
