The full step loop in one register: Plan, Do, Reflect, and the universal + that feeds them

This canonical picks up where plan-tab-step-integration-canonical.html ended (decisions D1–D4) and adds two new decisions plus the missing two thirds of the step loop. D5 demotes AI Coach from a full purple card to a quiet one-line helper — most users will fill the plan manually, and the empty state should feel writable, not guided. D6 turns the top-header + button into a universal capture sheet that handles quick-capture, blueprint-import, social-import, concept-drop, and share — five entries to one surface. Below: Plan revised in the empty & partial state, Do current vs target, Reflect current vs target, the + sheet rendered as a state, and a three-phase flow diagram tracing how the state pill carries the user through Planned → Live · capturing → Activity complete → Reflect · open → Settled.

1 · The state pill is the user's compass.

Across the three tabs the same step card carries five state-pill colors: gray Planned, blue Current step, coral Live · capturing, green Activity complete, purple Reflect · ready, green Settled. The user reads color first, label second. If they remember nothing else about the product, they remember "the colored thing in the corner tells me where I am."

2 · AI Coach is help, not direction.

The empty plan today says "Let AI structure your thoughts." The new register says "Here are three questions. Start writing. AI is around if you get stuck." The shift is from recommendation-first to writing-first, helper-on-demand. Materially this is one quiet line at the top of Plan body plus a per-field ✦ spark — both retire to More options once the plan has content.

quiet helper · per-field spark · self-retiring

3 · The + is universal, not single-purpose.

One button, five entry-points, one ranked surface. The quick-capture composer at the top of the sheet handles the 90% case (idea-out-fast); four secondary rows handle blueprint-import, social-import, concept-drop, share. The sheet itself is the same iOS sheet pattern Notes and Messages use — familiar physics, custom content.

quick capture · blueprint · follow · concept · share

4 · Reflect is prose, not score.

The star rating retires. The two text fields stay but in italic serif at 14/1.45 — the same vocabulary as Debrief's reflection body, just at smaller scale. The synthesis affordance at the top is the AI's polite offer: "want me to draft this from what you said out loud?" The user keeps full control of what's written.

serif italic · synthesis prompt · coral mic · save & settle

5 · The bottom CTA carries the loop.

Plan ends with Next: Start Doing → (blue, disabled until what). Do ends with Stop capturing (coral border, ghost). Reflect ends with Save & settle (blue, primary). Three CTAs, one per phase, each one carrying the user to the next state. The phase tabs at top remain free-jump tappable for the user who wants to go backward — but forward is always the bottom CTA.

blue → coral → blue
