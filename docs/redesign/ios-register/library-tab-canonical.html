AddendumFollowing individuals · suggest bar · everything adoptable

Two new pieces of plumbing land in this addendum. People become a first-class Library zone alongside Plans, Concepts, and Resources — you can follow a sailor without sharing a plan, and you should see their timeline in the same place you see plans you've subscribed to. Suggestions get a home of their own — they're inputs to doing, not to understanding, so they live in Practice as an Inbox above the timeline. And the rule that pulls it all together: every step a user can see is adoptable — there's never a viewable step in this product that lacks an Add to my timeline affordance.

D26 · People as a fourth Library zone

Library now has four zones: Plans · People · Concepts · Resources. Plans + People = the network you've assembled. Concepts + Resources = the knowledge you've gathered.

D27 · Suggest bar lives in Practice

An Inbox icon in the Practice top-header opens a unified surface with three sources: suggestions from followees/mentors, new steps from plans you're subscribed to, and items you saved to deck yourself. A purple inbox-strip above the timeline previews the count when non-empty.

D28 · Every step is adoptable

Any step the user can see — plan step, subscriber step, followee step, suggested step, an item on deck — surfaces an Add this step to my timeline affordance. Single shared <AdoptStepFooter> component; provenance preserved.

D29 · Two timeline scopings

A followee's timeline opens in their whole timeline by default — grouped by plan (one block per plan, plus a "Solo steps" block). When opened via a plan's Subscribers tab, it opens scoped to that plan only — segmented control flips between the two.

D30 · Suggesting is a step menu action

From the step card menu (⋮), users with mutual follows see Suggest this step to…. Sheet rises with recent followees. Sent suggestion lands in the recipient's Inbox with full provenance ("Suggested by Sam Cooke · 2 days ago").

D31 · Timelines are horizontal step cards

Every timeline in the product — yours, a subscriber's, a followee's, a plan's authored sequence — is a horizontal scroll of step cards with a NOW divider in the middle. Done to the left, planned to the right. Your own cards are long-press-to-reorder; others' are read-only. No vertical lists.

The People zone is exactly the missing piece for people you don't share a plan with — coaches you watch, sailors at other clubs whose work you respect.

Step interiorInside a step · four phase tabs · Plan answers five fundamentals · Library threads into sub-steps · Discuss when shared

A step is the unit of doing. Every step has four phase tabs: Plan · Do · Reflect · Discuss. Plan answers five questions — What you'll do, Why, How (the substantive part — sub-steps), with Who, Where — plus the capabilities this step will develop. How is where Library hooks back into action: a sub-step can be a plain todo ("hold the high lane for 90s"), or a resource link ("read Ch 7 of Sail Trim Theory"), or a concept-application ("apply 'Trust the shift, not just the side'"). Library becomes loadable into the step plan. Discuss appears as a tab when the step has shared access — a subscribed plan (Kevin + other subscribers), a suggestion (the suggester), or a collaborator in With. Solo, self-only steps don't get a Discuss tab.

D32 · Four phase tabs

Every step has Plan · Do · Reflect · Discuss. Discuss is conditional (see D35). The other three are always present even on solo steps.

D33 · Plan = five fundamentals + capabilities

What you'll do · Why · How · With who · Where · plus the capabilities the step develops. Each is its own card in Plan body. AI Coach spark (✦) per card.

D34 · Library threads into How sub-steps

Sub-steps inside How can be plain todos, resource links (amber chip), or concept applications (purple chip). Library content lives inline in the step plan — checking it off completes the sub-step and marks the concept as Tested.

D35 · Discuss conditional on shared access

Discuss tab shows up when the step has >1 person with access: subscribed plan (subscribers can talk), suggestion (suggester + recipient), or a collaborator named in With. Solo steps hide the tab.

1 · Answers, in one sentence each.

Where do subscribed plans live? Library → Plans zone.

Where do other subscribers' timelines live? Inside the plan → Subscribers tab.

Where do followed people's timelines live? Library → People zone (new fourth zone). Tap a person → their whole timeline, grouped by plan.

Where does the Suggest Bar live? In Practice, as an Inbox icon in the top-header (red-dot count) + optional purple strip above the timeline. Tap → Inbox screen with three sources: people · plans · on-deck.

Rename Playbook → Library? Yes — Library accommodates Plans, People, Concepts, and Resources. Playbook was too narrow.

Is every step adoptable? Yes — single shared <AdoptStepFooter>. No viewable step in this product lacks an Add to my timeline affordance.

2 · What changes for the user.

The zoomed-out Practice timeline gets cleaner — only your steps, but each carries the plan's stripe + chip. Plans no longer interleave with your own work; instead, they're visibly tagged onto your own work.

Subscribed plans, followed people, your concepts, and your saved resources all live in Library — the one long-arc memory layer. Plans + People are who you walk with; Concepts + Resources are what you've kept.

Suggestions land in an Inbox in Practice — never in Library. Reasoning: a suggestion is an input to doing, not understanding. The Inbox unifies three previously-scattered concepts (followee suggestions, plan-pushes, on-deck items) into one queue with one set of actions.

The Playbook concept-refinement loop is preserved verbatim. D18–D20 carry forward. The "Co-practitioners" sub-destination from network-timelines-canonical retires — it's now Subscribers inside the relevant plan.

3 · Open questions for you.

People vs. Plans ordering. Plans first or People first in Library? I have Plans first (it's the active path); People second (the relationships supporting it). Worth checking against how users describe their library to a friend.

Suggest sheet from a step. D30 says suggesting is a step-menu action. Should the sheet be a quick avatar grid (recent followees), or a full picker with search across all followees + crew? I'd start with the avatar grid + "More…" row.

Plan-push routing. When a coach unlocks a new step in a plan you're subscribed to, should it (a) auto-add to Next up in your timeline, or (b) land in your Inbox awaiting your call? Mockup shows option (b) — slower but respectful. Could be a per-plan setting.

Mentor designation. "Mentor" badge in §6 is heavier than "Follow" — is there a formal mentor role, or is it just "someone you follow whose suggestions auto-elevate"? Worth deciding before the People zone ships.

Inbox strip persistence. Should the purple strip in §8B stay until inbox is empty, or fade after first view of the day? My instinct: fade after view, reappear on new item.
