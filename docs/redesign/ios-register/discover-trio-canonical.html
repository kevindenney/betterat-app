Surface 3 · Forums

The unit is the topic

Topic name, broader interest, thread count, last activity. Topics are ongoing spaces, not single threads; the count is what travels at-a-glance. Joined topics carry a Following tag; the small coral dot is the unread mark. Coral never escalates to a row tint.

What makes these siblings

Three views into one shelf, not three different shelves.

The chrome is identical at the structural level — large-title Discover, three-segment switcher, single search field, system-gray-6 ground, grouped white inset list at 16px margins, the floating tab bar. The cell’s scaffolding is identical too: 44px leading mark, 16px name, 13px descriptor, optional secondary line, right-edge tag, disclosure chevron. What changes is the unit: institution, person, topic.

The shared rules: one search field with three placeholders, never three different fields; one tag grammar with three roles (mine / weak / fit) used identically across all three surfaces; one coral semantic, reserved across the trio for system attention only. The list of differences is the cell’s third row — concept on People, activity on Forums, omitted on Orgs — and the leading mark’s shape: square for institution, round for person, square-with-glyph for topic.

Cell density tradeoff

Heights size to content; rhythm comes from the scaffold.

Position taken: the Orgs cell has two lines (name, descriptor); the People cell has three (name, context, current concept); the Forums cell has three (name, interest, activity). Heights are not forced equal. Forcing them would add fake padding to the Orgs cell or crush the Forums cell’s activity line — both dishonest.

Visual rhythm is preserved by what is constant: the 12 px inner padding, the 44 px leading mark column, the 16 px name size, the 0.5 px hairline between cells, the right-edge tag column. The eye reads the column structure as repeating even when the row heights differ — the same way iOS Settings reads as a single list even though some rows have subtitles and others don’t.

Signal hierarchy on People

Current concept is what travels at a glance.

Choice: of the three available evidence signals (latest reflection title, current concept, recent trophy of becoming), the cell shows the current concept. Defence:

Latest reflection is the most recent atom — useful in a profile, noisy in a list. The reflection title might be anything from “Tuesday night was windy” to a deep technical note; the reader of a Discover list can’t tell which without tapping in.

Trophy of becoming is past tense — what they were good at last season. It reads as a credential, which sits poorly with a deliberate-practice frame where the point is what you’re trying to get good at now.

Current concept answers the only question a practitioner asks at the moment of discovery: “is this person working on something I care about?” A shared concept is the highest-information predictor that a follow will be worth it.

Forums — thread or topic?

Topic. Ongoing spaces, not single Q&A threads.

The position is encoded in the cell. The cell shows thread count (1,184 threads) and last activity (17 new today) — the activity meaningful for a topic, not a thread. A thread cell would show the original post, the author, and the reply count to that thread. We don’t do that here.

Why: a discover-of-threads frame turns the trio into a feed. The brief explicitly rejects feed grammar (“not feed-style”). Single-question Q&A is one shape conversation takes inside a topic; sometimes a topic is mostly Q&A (rules & protests), sometimes it’s mostly storytelling (heavy-weather race reports). The unit Discover names is the space, not the conversation.

The downstream Topic detail surface is where threads live as a list, and that surface inherits its grammar from this one.

Where the unread / new-activity indicator lives

A small coral dot in the trailing column, only on joined topics.

Position taken: unread is a state that applies only to joined topics. A discoverable topic isn’t “unread” in any meaningful sense — the user has no relationship to it yet. The coral dot appears in the ctail column, below the Following tag, sized to match the live-dot grammar in the running state and the accent-dot grammar on Paths-for-you. Same component, same halo, three different jobs the user reads as “the system wants to point at this”.

Numbers (17 new today) live inside the cell’s activity row in label-secondary — informational, not attention-grabbing. The coral dot is the only thing that says “something here moved since you last looked.”

Already mine vs. undiscovered

Mixed in one list, marked with a small relationship tag.

Position taken: one list, not two sections. The user’s own org sits in the same scroll position as the federation it belongs to; the people they follow sit in the same scroll as the people they’ve been suggested. The differentiator is the small right-edge tag (Joined, Following, Mutual) and the absence of any other visual escalation — no bolding, no background tint, no extra padding.

Defence: separating mine-from-new produces two surfaces glued together with a section header, which then competes with the segmented control for the role of “the thing that changes the view”. Mixing keeps the segmented control as the only switcher; relationship is a property of a cell, not the framing of a region. The user who only wants their own can tap into Profile.

Sibling to the loading and error registers

Three states of one surface — loading, empty, error — same hand.

Discover surfaces will be sometimes slow (recommendation compute on People) and sometimes failed (network on any of them). The loading register plugs in as the existing Get Inspired running sheet pattern, adapted: a small narration line above the list while results compute (“Finding sailors who share your concepts.”). The error register is the canonical full-screen surface, with the back chevron returning to the segmented control intact.

The empty state inset shown on the Forums phone is the third corner of the same triangle — same muted glyph, same plain sentence, same single quiet action. Loading narrates, empty offers, error names what failed; the grammar is identical across all three.

What this trio explicitly is not

  • Not detail surfacesOrg page, Person page, Topic page are downstream. This brief is the index level. Tap targets are designed; what’s on the other side of the tap isn’t.
  • Not feed-styleNo timestamps as the leading signal. No chronological mixing of orgs / people / threads. Discover is a shelf of units, not a stream of activity.
  • Not algorithmically opinionatedThe unit is the hero. Recommendation is one signal on the unit (the coral fit tag); it never frames the surface. No Recommended for you chrome.
  • Not faculty densityPatricia doesn’t browse Discover. This is practitioner-side; standard iOS density.
  • Not the follow / join modalThe tap target leads to a detail surface; the actual subscription interaction is one tap deeper. Affordance shown, modal not designed.
  • No coral on relationship tagsCoral is for system attention (matches your concept, unread). Joined / Following / Mutual are gray. Coral never gets the “I belong here” job, which would burn its single semantic.
  • No counts in redUnread counts are gray-secondary text; the coral dot is the only thing that escalates. Numbers don’t pretend to be alarms.
  • No empty illustrationThe empty-state inset uses the same 36px muted glyph the error register uses. No mascot. No cartoon. The sentence carries the surface.
