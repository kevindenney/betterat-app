The subscriber detail surface — where a creator actually mentors

A continuation of the Blueprint Creator Dashboard canonical: the screens a creator sees after tapping View progress on a single subscriber. The creator on screen is Bram van Olphen; the subscriber is Tomás Renart — a Dragon-class racer in Hong Kong, nine steps into Bram’s 14-step Dragon Worlds Prep blueprint. This is the surface where mentoring at scale stops being analytics and becomes a relationship.

Four frames, ordered by gravity: Progress is the landing surface — the at-a-glance — but the work happens on Reflect Feed. Messages and Notes flank it. Privacy is structural: Notes are private to Bram and the chrome says so; Messages are a two-way thread; Reflect comments are visible to Tomás by default and labelled as such on the affordance.

Frame 4 · Notes — the private layer

Notes · the page in the back of the binder

A creator’s working memory: candid observations, follow-ups, the things you’d write in a coaching binder. The privacy strip across the top is amber rather than blue — it’s a signal, not an action — and the tab label itself shows a padlock instead of an eye icon. Each note carries a date, a chip or two linking it to specific steps or blueprints, and a soft cream card that visually distances Notes from the white feed cards that Tomás can see. The keyboard shortcut hint (N) is the only desktop affordance that won’t survive the mobile-responsive collapse.

Mobile-responsive degradation exists — the four inner tabs become a horizontal scroller, the summary card stacks vertically, the Reflect-feed thread bubbles widen to fill the width, and the message composer pins to the bottom of the viewport — but the canonical view is desktop. A creator mentoring twenty-eight subscribers does this work at a desk, not on a phone.

Why Reflect Feed gets the most ink

Three of the four frames are chrome with content; this is the one where the work happens. The card is bigger, the type is softer (Iowan serif on the answers — same family the consumer-facing reflection screens use), and Bram’s comment lives inside the card rather than below it, threaded with Tomás’s replies. The card containing the un-replied reflection picks up a 2px iOS-blue outline and a coloured call-out strip; we want it to be the thing your eye lands on first.

The affordances row deliberately mixes verbs: Comment (open), Evidence · Starts (already marked, a small win), Mark capability, Request more detail. Marking a reflection as capability evidence is the only place in the product where the creator directly drives the subscriber’s capability bars upward — and the green pill is the receipt.

Privacy is structural, not chromatic

Three different privacy postures live on this surface and the chrome makes each one explicit. Reflect comments are public-to-Tomás — every affordance row carries an eye · Visible to Tomás line on the right. Messages are two-way private — the iOS-blue bubbles are unmistakable. Notes are creator-only — amber privacy strip, padlock icon on the tab, an Only you can see this tab hint where the “What Tomás sees” affordance would normally sit.

The amber on the Notes strip is the same amber used for stall flags and stuck points. We treat it as the “handle with care” color: something you should read before you act.

The breadcrumb is the back button

The strap row that used to say Creator Dashboard / Bram van Olphen · 5 blueprints… on the dashboard canonical now reads ← Subscribers on the left and the subscriber’s identity on the right. The shape is preserved — 56px, same icon-button cluster on the right — but the gravity moves to a person. The bulk-mentoring affordance (Message all) is cited only; it lives one level up in the Subscribers list. From a subscriber detail you are talking to one person.

The desktop URL — betterat.app/creator/subscribers/tomas-renart/… — encodes the subscriber slug at the path level and the tab at the leaf. Deep-link from a notification (Step 7 reflection · awaiting your comment) lands directly on the Reflect Feed tab with Step 7 expanded.

What got skipped

Bulk mentoring — cohort-wide messaging, multi-select on the Subscribers list — is cited in the parent canonical and not rendered here. From inside one person’s detail it would only confuse the gravity.

Mentoring monetization tiers (paid mentor minutes, “office hours,” one-on-one upsell) are a deferred product decision and don’t appear on this surface. Voice and video messaging are out of scope; the composer is text-and-attach only.

Subscriber-side equivalents — what Tomás sees when Bram comments on his reflection, when Bram sends him a message — are part of the iOS Register canonicals and stay there.
