BetterAt · Phase P · Welcome banner · Canonical — 2026-05-16

The welcome banner that greets a sailor on /practice the first time they arrive following Kevin's blueprint

Auto-follow is a meaningful relationship — the sailor's Plan tab will now be shaped by Kevin's fourteen steps, and Kevin will see their reflections. The banner is the only chance to name that relationship at the moment it forms. It's dismissible, non-modal — the practice surface beneath remains fully usable — but visually distinct from generic toast / snackbar patterns because this is not a transient notification, it's a moment.

Coexistence note. Per the corrected Phase P spec this banner is additive on /practice — it does not replace the existing HKDWWelcomeCard on /community/2027-hk-dragon-worlds or the BlueprintWelcomeCard on /(tabs)/races. Three welcome cards on three surfaces is acceptable for the May 20 launch; consolidation is post-demo work and out of scope here.

Banner anatomy · 1.4×

KD

Welcome to BetterAt

You're now following Kevin's Dragon Worlds 2027 blueprint.

Tap any step below to start planning. Your reflections will be shared with Kevin.

  1. 1

    Kevin's avatar with a green check. The check is the visual cue that the auto-follow succeeded — and ties the banner back to the Follow the blueprint button on the landing page. Kevin's identity is rendered as a name + avatar, never as a logo or blueprint glyph.

  2. 2

    Eyebrow + title + body, not toast copy. The title is set in serif at 17px, three lines max — it reads as editorial, not as a system notification. The blueprint name is italicised; Kevin's is bold-weight to anchor the relationship.

  3. 3

    Two dismiss paths. The primary Got it button is the explicit acknowledgement — it's iOS-blue because it ties to the same affordance the user just tapped on the landing page. The X in the top-right is the implicit dismiss for sailors who skim and move on.

  4. 4

    A blue accent strip at the left edge ties the banner visually to the /redeem hero they came from, without using a saturated background that would compete with the step card beneath. The banner reads as inert-but-distinct.

  5. 5

    A second action: View blueprint. The brief didn't ask for it, but the moment is right — many sailors will want to see what they just opted into before they tap a step. Routes to /blueprint/dragon-worlds-2027-peak-performance. Tappable but visually quiet so it doesn't compete with Got it.

Design notes

The five jobs the banner does at once

This banner is not just a welcome — it's the moment a sailor first realises they're in a relationship with Kevin's authoring. Five things have to be true at the same time, or it falls flat.

1. Names the relationship

“You're now following Kevin's Dragon Worlds 2027 blueprint” — explicit subject (you), explicit author (Kevin), explicit artefact (the blueprint). Not “welcome to BetterAt” in isolation; not “blueprint added” in isolation.

2. Renders Kevin as a person

Kevin's avatar with a green check, not a blueprint cover or a generic following icon. The first time a sailor reads the banner they should think I'm following a human, not I'm following a course.

3. Stays out of the way

Non-modal, scroll-along, dismissible. The step card beneath is fully tappable from the first frame. Sailors who want to dive into the first step can — they don't have to dismiss anything first.

4. Sets expectation about visibility

“Your reflections will be shared with Kevin” — the one sentence in the banner most likely to be re-read. The mentoring relationship goes both ways and the banner is the only surface that says so before a sailor types a word.
