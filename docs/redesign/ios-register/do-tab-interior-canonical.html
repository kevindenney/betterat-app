Coral only when it's live

The LIVE · IN PROGRESS coral pulse is the only place coral appears at full chroma on the Do surface. The instant the activity ends, coral retreats to chips, flag pills, and the inferred-capability accent — never the active phase. This makes "the activity is happening right now" a perceptual signal, not a label the user has to read.

iOS coral · pulsing dot in phase tab + step label

Reverse-chronological by default

Mid-activity, the newest capture is at the top — like iMessage or Twitter. Sailors glance at the most recent thing they said. Each row carries a left-border accent indicating type: blue for voice, gray for typed, coral for silent flags. Voice transcripts render in italics; typed notes are plain. A capability chip on the right shows AI's best guess and is tap-to-edit.

voice · typed · flagged

Floating mic is persistent

Once captures exist, a single floating mic anchors the bottom-right of the body — smaller than the Frame-1 trio, but always present. It coral-pulses while the activity is live, then becomes a steady iOS blue when the session ends. The Frame-1 trio is for starting; the floating mic is for continuing without breaking flow.

live · post-activity

Auto-summary is a handoff to Reflect

The Frame-3 narrative card is not the end-state — it's the bridge. By the time the user lands on the post-activity Do view, the AI has already produced a 3-4 sentence reading of what just happened, complete with the user's "stickiest" moment surfaced. The full-width Move to Reflect → CTA closes the loop. The Reflect phase tab pulses quiet blue as the next step.
