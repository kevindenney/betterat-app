The Becoming Loop: how Plan, Do, Reflect, the hinges between them, and the people you practice with feed your Profile of Becoming

This canonical names what was implicit across the prior twelve passes: capabilities are the spine of the product, not a feature on one tab. Plan tags what a step is working toward; Do captures the evidence; Reflect confirms it with a strength rating; Profile aggregates it into a capability map; and the long-arc surface called Profile of Becoming renders it as a single sketchbook arc across the user's entire practice life. Between every two steps a hinge names the time that filled the gap — the design for which already exists. Around every step sit the people you practice with — your crew, your fleet, your cohort — with whom you can share single observations one capture at a time.

Three new decisions land here. D10 makes the capability spine visible end-to-end. D11 brings the existing hinge design into the new register and wires it to the loop's transitions. D12 adds suggestion rows from your network on Plan, a co-practice WITH row on every step, and a per-capture share sheet with three rings of visibility.

ShareCapture-level share sheet · three rings of visibility, per capture, never per step

Long-press any capture in the Do stream — or tap its share affordance — and a sheet rises with three rings of visibility. Private stays the default; Crew opens to the people on your boat or your nursing study-group; Fleet / Cohort opens to everyone who raced this race or did this rotation. Sharing is per-capture, not per-step — a sailor can post a single observation to the fleet without making the whole reflection public. Nursing inherits a redaction note when patient identifiers are detected.

1 · Capabilities are the spine.

Every surface in the product is in service of capability evidence accumulating. Plan tags (intent). Do captures (raw material). Reflect confirms with strength (judgment). Profile maps (current state). Of Becoming arcs (biography). Same capability_id threads all five — the user reads color to know which node they're standing in.

2 · The hinge is the system's most honest surface.

Existing locked design: lower weight than a step card (no shadow, regular 400 title), filmstrip of named days, three named kinds, bookends below, phrasing varies with gap length. New here: amber state pill Between · settling distinguishes it from the five step states. The hinge is a state, not a destination — the user arrives by swipe in Phase 2; in Phase 1 by tapping the timeline icon.

filmstrip · 3 kinds · bookends

3 · Suggestions from the network sit on the work, not beside it.

Three quiet rows on Plan — blueprint, follow, mentor. Each one a "you might add this next" affordance with provenance. Tap → preview the source step → optionally add to your timeline. The universal + sheet (D6) is for the ad-hoc add; this is the in-context add. Both surfaces feed the same capture-step mechanism, ranked by user attention rather than rank by completeness.

blueprint follow mentor

4 · Practice is with people, not in isolation.

The WITH row makes the social dimension of the step visible from the moment the user opens it. Sailing: crew avatars + fleet chip. Nursing: cohort chip. Drawing: skip the row entirely if no one's with you. The row is quiet — it sits under the title and doesn't compete with the field-cards or the phase tabs. Its job is to remind you that what you're about to do is shareable, and with whom.

crew avatars · fleet chip · cohort chip

5 · Sharing is per capture, never per step.

A reflection is private by default. A single observation can post to the fleet without changing the reflection's visibility. Three rings: Private (you + coach), Crew (your boat / study-group), Fleet / Cohort (everyone who raced this race / did this rotation). Nursing gets a PII scrubber before any cohort post. The user never has to choose share all or share nothing; the unit of decision is the capture.

private crew fleet/cohort

6 · "Of Becoming" is the long-arc name.

One unifying phrase across two scales. Trophy of Becoming at path-scale, already canonical. Profile of Becoming at lifetime-scale, new. Same sketchbook arc grammar, same amber wash, same restraint. When the user sees either, they read the same idea: this is what you've become, in your own evidence, in your own line.
