Reflect before there's anything to reflect on — a calm, honest empty state that points the user back to Do

The user has tapped Reflect early — Plan is set, but Do hasn't started yet (zero captures, zero voice). The Reflect tab refuses to pretend. There's no AI-drafted summary, no fake prompts, no hollow celebration. Instead the surface admits its state: a single restrained empty card explains why the tab is quiet, and two ghost rows below sketch the future shape of the screen — dashed-outline placeholders where real prompts will land once captures exist. A single secondary affordance ("Jot a freeform note instead") preserves the user's option to reflect anyway, in case the activity happened off-app.

Tone: quiet and procedural. The primary CTA is iOS-blue and points back to Do — the system's honest opinion about what comes next. The footer hint names what's missing in plain numbers ("0 captures · 0 voice") so the empty state feels measured, not vague.

Why empty needs its own frame

Most reflective tools quietly hide their UI when there's no data, or worse, fake one with mock prompts. Both are wrong here. The Reflect tab is a phase of a practice loop — if it's empty, that's information. Showing the surface as-shaped-but-empty teaches the user the loop's grammar before they ever populate it.

Hence the two ghost rows: not decoration, previews. They name what the future will hold (a What worked row and a What to improve row) without putting words in the user's mouth.

gray-3 dashed · ghost rows

The honest CTA

The biggest temptation in an empty state is to invent something for the user to do right here: "Add your first reflection," "Start journaling," etc. We resist. The whole point of Reflect is that it follows Do — pretending the user can reflect without captures dilutes the loop.

So the CTA points back: Open Do tab. The freeform-note option exists as a secondary affordance for the rare case (activity happened off-app, user wants a journal entry anyway), but it's not the headline.

ios-blue · primary CTA points to Do

Quiet, not sad

An empty state can read as failure ("you haven't done it!") or as opportunity ("you're about to start"). The framing is in the tone: the title is descriptive, not pleading ("Nothing to reflect on yet" vs. "You haven't reflected!"). The body uses warm second-person verbs. The footer hint trusts the user — "this tab will fill itself in once captures arrive" — rather than scolding them.

The radial green-wash in the empty hero card is the smallest tell that this is still the Reflect surface. Brand signature preserved; mood kept neutral.
