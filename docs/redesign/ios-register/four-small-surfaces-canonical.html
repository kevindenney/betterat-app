Four small surfaces, one reference

Mechanical UI that doesn't justify its own canonical, gathered into one doc: the interest switcher from the top of Practice, the profile / settings dropdown from the avatar, the share dialog from any step, and the Suggest bar — the surface where steps from people you follow surface as candidates for your timeline. The first three are iOS-native action sheets and popovers; the Suggest bar gets two frames so its spatial relationship to Following and the user's own timeline is unambiguous.

Frames 1 — 3

iOS-native action sheets and popovers

These three behave like standard iOS components — frosted-glass blur, system fonts, system button styling. The Practice tab sits behind them, dimmed; tapping outside dismisses. They are deliberately not styled as BetterAt surfaces — they should feel like the OS, not the app.

Your timeline dominates the top — the user's own steps, in order, with the current one outlined in iOS blue. This is the page's center of gravity.

Following is a thin peer band — small cards, dashed hairline above, gray accent. Tells the user what people they follow are working on right now, but at a glance — not a feed.

Frame 5 · Desktop zoomed-out context (1440px target, rendered at scale) The spatial sequence is the design: you → following → suggested reads top-to-bottom without any labels. Per-source filtering and per-card author UI are deferred — this canonical only fixes the bar's place in the page.

Why these four, together

Each surface is too small to deserve its own canonical — but each is referenced by other canonicals. Bundling them here means one place to point to when the Practice or Step-detail canonicals say “and then the share sheet.”

The Suggest bar gets two frames because its meaning is positional — it's defined by what it sits next to.

iOS-native vs. BetterAt-styled

Frames 1–3 are deliberately styled as the OS. Frosted blur, system fonts, system buttons. They should disappear into the user's muscle memory.

The Suggest bar (Frames 4–5) is the opposite: it's a BetterAt-specific surface and gets its own tint (warm cream) and accent (coral header) so it's never confused with the user's own timeline.

Suggest bar — surface rules

One: every card has Add and Dismiss, never anything else at this level. Two: cards are candidates, not feed items — they don't accumulate, they get acted on. Three: a dismissed card never re-surfaces from the same source within a week.

Suggestion-author UI (who is allowed to suggest, what they see when they do) lives in the Mentoring canonical when that lands.

Out of scope

Each Settings sub-surface in the profile popover

Suggestion-author UI (Mentoring canonical)
