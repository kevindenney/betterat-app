One Atlas, three vantage points — how the mentor & org side works

Why a companion, not a rebuild

  • #29 STANDSThe entrepreneur's four frames (her circuit, the haat cockpit, her Lakhpati health, her SHG-scoped Nearby) are unchanged. These four are the same Atlas seen by the people who mentor her.
  • ROLE-GATEDBetterAt already has the four-tier social model (interests · orgs · programs · people). A user's role in the org (didi vs CRP/Sakhi vs CLF coordinator) decides which Atlas vantage they get — same data, different altitude.
  • REGISTERMentor/org frames shift the accent terra → indigo to signal "you're acting with org authority," and the tab row gains a दीदी / Mentees entry that only mentors see.

Three altitudes of the same "where" lens

Didi (#29)Her own livelihood circuit — one home, one haat, her savings. "What do I do this week?"
CRP / Sakhi (E·F·H)A cluster of SHGs she mentors. Who needs a visit, one nudge each, log the visit as evidence. "Who do I help, and how?"
CLF / JSLPS (G)The whole Lakhpati cohort across villages. Crossing-₹1-lakh coverage, scheme uptake, laggard villages. "Is the program working?"

Who the mentors actually are (real DAY-NRLM cadres)

  • CRP / Sakhi — Community Resource Person & the Aajeevika / Bank / Krishi / Pashu Sakhi cadres: village women, already trusted, already paid to mentor. Frame E/F/H is built for the phone in their hand.
  • VO → CLF coordinator — the Village Organization and Cluster Level Federation staff under JSLPS (Jharkhand State Livelihood Promotion Society, the state DAY-NRLM mission). Frame G is their MIS.
  • Schemes as Programs — Lakhpati Didi, PMFME (food-processing subsidy), Mudra credit, FSSAI licensing. The didi subscribes; the mentor routes her to them; the org tracks uptake.

Primitive mapping (the mentor side)

  • Mentoring visit = a Step with a who (the didi), a where (her village/haat), and evidence (the note + the blueprint step assigned). It rolls up the SHG → VO → CLF federation as real data.
  • A nudge = a routed Blueprint step. When the mentor assigns "try Bundu haat," it lands in the didi's own plan (the Plan-is-a-menu pattern) — the two sides share one timeline.
  • Frame G coverage = aggregated Competency/Evidence. "86 of 240 crossed ₹1 lakh" and scheme-uptake bars are the cohort roll-up of the same sales/savings/licence evidence each didi logs — not a separate report.

Same real-user constraints as #29

  • VOICEMentors are village women too — 🎙️ dictate the visit note, 🔊 read-aloud everywhere, big touch targets, vernacular (Hindi shown; Santhali/Sadri/Mundari/Kurukh on localization).
  • EDGECRPs walk between villages with patchy signal — the cluster map, roster and visit log cache offline and sync later; ₹ & UPI-native throughout.
  • TRUSTA mentor sees a didi because she's in their assigned SHG cluster, never anonymous distance — matching how JSLPS/DAY-NRLM actually assigns CRPs.

Build order (still isolated from sailing & nursing)

  • E5 — Role gating: resolve org-role → Atlas vantage (didi / CRP / CLF). Reuses the membership-role plumbing; the heavy lift is the new mentor frames, not new data.
  • E6 — Cluster map & roster (Frames E·F) · E7 — Log-a-visit loop + blueprint routing (Frame H, the keystone — it's what makes mentoring evidenced) · E8 — CLF cohort dashboard (Frame G).
  • Data caveat: needs the SHG→VO→CLF org tree with CRP-to-SHG assignments, a didi status signal (derivable from sales/savings cadence), and a scheme catalog. Verify what a Jharkhand demo can seed before building — this completes the rural-Indian-entrepreneur slot in the per-persona Atlas rollout with its mentor/org half.
