Step — WHERE · WHO · Review connective tissue
How the Plan-tab WHERE and WHO fields become persona-native, and how they (plus capabilities and the season) all cash out in Review. Row A = where-pickers, Row B = role-typed who-pickers, Row C = the Review payoff.
The loop Plan sets a where (a craft-native spatial object), a who (role-typed — one of them a validator), and a capability. Do captures evidence at that where, with those who, tagged to that capability. Review is where all three converge: the where becomes a comparison axis, the validator co-signs, the capability grows, and it all rolls up to the season goal. Season is light in Plan, silent in Do, and the payoff in Review.

WHERE and WHO aren't generic form fields — they're typed objects whose payoff is realized in Review and the season.

1Review is reflection-first. The user still answers four generalized questions, identical for every interest and every step (what worked · where to practice · the one thing to remember · what evidence you'd ask a learner for) — exactly the ritual the current app has. Pulled-forward data never replaces the answer; it seeds each question and lands below as payoff.
2WHO is role-typed, not just tagged. Co-doers (positions) · a validator · the group. The validator's "signs off" flag is the only thing that wires a person into Review's capability confirmation — so the field has to capture role, not just name.
3Capabilities are used only where they earn it. Set lightly in Plan (a chip), grown silently by Do captures, and confirmed in Review with evidence + co-sign. If a step builds no capability, the chip is absent — never a forced field.
4Season is weighted by phase. A thin context line in Plan ("why this step now"), silent in Do, and the payoff band in Review — the one place a single step visibly moves the bigger goal. Same concept, persona vocab (rotation / series / selling season / championship run).
