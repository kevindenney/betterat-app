What this combines — and the rules

L1 + L2 become one Step level; the ladder drops to three; Add step gets a real picker.
1One card is the step AND its place in time. The card fills ~80% of the screen and holds the full L1 detail (phase arc, what/how, sub-steps, phase action). The previous + next cards peek at the edges — that's the old L2 carousel, sized up.
2An obvious NOW bar, kept from L2. A vertical orange marker divides the done peek (muted-green, left) from the active card + planned peek (dashed, right). The top NOW row labels the time + position; the pager shows the NOW tick. Swiping moves through the sequence.
3Three zoom levels now: Step · Arc · All. Collapsing L1+L2 means the ladder loses a rung. The middle label stays persona-native (Arc / Rotation / Season).
4No Librarian or noticing here. This level is for doing the step in front of you. The Librarian's observations + "review this arc" live one level up (Arc) and at All — never on the Step card.
5Add step is a real picker, not just a blank. The dialog offers: a blank step, your subscribed blueprints (grouped with their adoptable steps), your ✨ suggestions (suggest bar), and ⇄ cross-interest steps. One ＋ per row.
6Adopted steps land by sort_order. Anything added from the dialog drops into the sequence to the right of NOW — selective, incremental, reorderable. Plan stays a menu, not a calendar.
