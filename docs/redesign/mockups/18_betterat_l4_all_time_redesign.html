BROWSE ARCS → Chapters — what changes, and why:
  • Persona-native section title. "BROWSE ARCS" (generic + jargon) becomes the craft's own word for a chapter — Sketchbooks for a drawer, Campaigns for a sailor, Rotations for a nurse — resolved from the same interestVocab the poster already uses.
  • Brick lanes → one representative strip. A row of ~16 individually-colored bricks per arc is undecodable at this scale and re-states the chips. Each card instead carries one thin mix strip (the arc's capability proportions as a single 5px bar) — visual identity without pretending each square is readable. The brick wall stays inside the ARC (L3) view, where week-granularity earns it.
  • One human subtitle, not three chips. "20 steps · mostly proportion · drew with Mira" says what "Proportion · 19 / 4 with people / 18/20 done" said, in a sentence you actually read.
  • The whole card is the tap target → ARC (L3). The chevron makes drill-in obvious; that's the one primary action a reflective list needs.
  • Editing is deferred. "+ New arc" and "Select" (and the per-lane ✏) move behind a single Edit button, the way Photos and Notes hide management until asked. The default state is for reading your practice, not administering it.

Tradeoff: cards are taller than brick lanes, so fewer chapters fit per screen. At lifetime scale you rarely have dozens of chapters, and "read each one in a glance" beats "twelve indecipherable brick rows." If a practice ever does accumulate many arcs, the cards collapse gracefully to title + date + strip.

What changes at the ALL altitude — and why:
  • Stop rescaling the L3 charts. Today adaptLifetimeForCharts() relabels weeks as "sessions" and feeds the same CapabilityMix river + PeerJourney dot-chart a longer ruler. With one arc those charts are nearly empty and the card literally apologizes. The river/peer-dot representation belongs to L3 (the season); ALL needs its own.
  • Lead with a poster, not a plot. Like Photos' "Years" hero, the lifetime opens with one serif sentence + 2–3 lifetime numbers. It answers "what is this craft to me" before any chart.
  • Arcs become chapters, not bricks-only. Each sketchbook is a node on a vertical spine with its dominant capability, dates, a one-line summary, and milestone. Tapping a chapter zooms to ARC (L3). The brick wall moves inside the ARC view where week-granularity matters.
  • The river only appears at 2+ arcs, and it's a drift river (how dominance shifted between arcs), not a per-week stack. One arc → no river at all.
  • People = constancy sentences. "Pat: 41 sessions across all 4 sketchbooks, since the first" is the genuine lifetime payoff — promote PeerConstancyList, drop the faint dot-chart that's unreadable at this scale.

Adaptive density (the two AFTER frames)

Same component, two states keyed off arc count: 1 arc → poster + single chapter + constancy (no river, no trajectory ribbon). 2+ arcs → poster + trajectory ribbon + drift river + full chapter spine. No empty charts, ever — the apology card disappears because the layout never out-runs the data.

Also fixes the live bug

The [BlueprintService] Failed to get suggested next steps toast (top of BEFORE) fires because the lifetime view requests next-step suggestions it doesn't need at this altitude. The redesign's ALL surface is reflective, not prescriptive — the suggestion call moves to WEEK/STEP where "what's next" is the actual question.
