CShelves (editorial). Most distinctive: the library reads like physical shelves — horizontal rows of cover-art plans, state-colored concept spines, file-kind tiles — scannable by sight, not by reading labels. A warm Yours band vs a muted Stacks band carries the spine without a label fighting for it. Librarian recedes to a floating ask bar + one insight ribbon.
My recommendation: ship B's structural moves (collapse the three intelligence surfaces into one librarian line; add a jump-back-in rail; make Yours/Stacks a real segment), borrowing A's paged librarian card for when there's a live insight worth a full action. Reserve C's shelf treatment for a later pass — it's the most opinionated and the most work (needs cover art / richer card data), but it's where the tab stops looking like a settings list and starts looking like a tool for the craft.
