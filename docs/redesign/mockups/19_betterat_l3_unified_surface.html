What makes this "one piece" instead of stacked widgets:
  • The arc is the log's filter handle. Today the capability chart, the peer lane and the BROWSE WEEKS list are three separate things. Here, tapping a capability chip or a person isolates the thread in the spine and filters the one step list below to exactly those steps — there's no second "drill-in" inset and no competing Sort/Capability/Select toolbar. The chart is the toolbar.
  • One shared spine. Bands → reflection strip → people lane share the identical week axis + NOW rule, read as a single annotated timeline. Hover scrubs one callout per week (mix + "you paused" + who).
  • The log is the single source of truth. Headline, chips, peer lane, reflection ticks, the "named the lasix" marker — all derive from the same step rows. Nothing is authored twice.
  • Two-way (forward ships first). Forward = tap chart → filter log (low risk, reuses one filter state). Reciprocal = hover a step row → its capability + people chips glow in the chart (shown here on hover). Forward is the commit; reciprocal is the follow-up.
  • Persona-native vocab throughout. "rotation", "preceptor", "the work", "cohort" for nursing; the same surface says "arc / crew / fleet" for a sailor, "sketchbook / studio" for a drawer — driven by the interest's vocab table, not hardcoded.
