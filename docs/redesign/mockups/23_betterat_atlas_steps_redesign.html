What changed from v22 (and what we kept)

The two corrections

  • FIXKeep the real top-down map with land. v22 leaned toward a dark schematic course canvas. But most steps happen ashore — the boatyard, the club, home — so the chart stays a real light geographic basemap (land + water + place labels), top-down, no 3D. Course geometry is drawn lightly on the actual water for racing steps, not as the whole canvas.
  • FIXThe cockpit is a step cockpit, not a race cockpit. Racing is one kind of step. The card keeps the v22 shell (kind eyebrow, title, when, drag-to-expand) and swaps the body by kind:
    • On-water (race / practice) → wind / current / sea gauges + favored-side strategy + per-third plan.
    • Boat work (tune / fix) → class target numbers + a tuning checklist with progress.
    • Learn (read / watch) → the resource + why-it-matters (same why capability link).

Kept from v22 (the look & feel you liked)

  • KEEPConsolidated top chrome — one search + filter row, Apple-Maps style. The filter now toggles step kinds (All / Boat work / Practice / Learn / Race) instead of race-only layers.
  • KEEPThe bottom cockpit card as the hero, drag-to-expand, with the same type scale, domain colors, and the green strategy block for on-water steps.
  • KEEPPressure-encoded wind over the water, slim next-step chip (now tinted by the next step's kind), and the fixed Nearby sheet — verbs already generalize.
  • KEEPFavored-side green + BOTTOM/MIDDLE/UPPER thirds for racing steps, and Atlas as the universal “where” lens — the kind-adaptive cockpit is what makes a nurse's clinical site or a runner's route fit the same frame later.

New in v23

  • NEWStep pins by kind — 🔧 boat work, 📖 learn, 🎯 practice, 🧭 coach, ⛵ race — so the map answers “where is everything I'm working on,” not just “where's the race.”
  • NEWKind-adaptive cockpit body — targets + checklist for land steps, gauges + strategy for water steps, resource for learning.
  • NEW“Why this step” capability link on every kind — ties the step (even a land chore like tuning) back to the capability it builds.
