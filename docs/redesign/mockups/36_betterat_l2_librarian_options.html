You flagged that my last pass dissolved the "Librarian noticed" card into the header + the adopt menu's subtitle — losing it as a distinct moment. Four ways to keep the Librarian — pick a direction

All share the docked ladder, the period header, the horizontal timeline + NOW bar, and a 3-row adopt menu. Only the Librarian treatment differs.
AStandalone card. Its own surface between timeline and menu, full voice + Not now / Review. Loudest, lowest-risk (closest to today). Cost: two stacked AI-ish surfaces, the thing you originally had two of.
BHeads the adopt menu. One card: noticing + choice on top, adoptable rows beneath. Keeps the full moment but merges the two surfaces with a clear notices→acts hand-off. My recommended default.
CCompact banner. One dismissible line under the header; "Review ›" expands it. Distinct but minimal — timeline stays the hero. Best when the Librarian shouldn't dominate every visit. Loses the serif persuasion.
DAnchored callout. Ties the observation to a literal "gap" slot on the track. Most concrete and teachable, but the most layout work and it pulls focus near the NOW bar.
