Feeds #29 Mockup #29 shows the money outputs — ₹6,400 this month, ₹900 last haat, the four income levers. Those numbers have to come from somewhere. These are the capture & tracking surfaces underneath: how a low-literacy, shared-phone user actually logs a sale, closes a market day, reads her बही-खाता (cash book), and tracks savings & her Mudra loan — without typing. Same design contract as #29: 🎙️ voice-first (speak the sale, AI parses it), 🔊 read-aloud, big touch targets, money in ₹, UPI-native, and an offline badge because the haat has no signal. Every number here is the one #29 displays — capture (A) → reconcile (B) → ledger (C) → savings/credit (D) is the full loop.

The money loop — capture → reconcile → track → save

Why these surfaces had to exist

  • #29 SHOWS#29 displays income, last-haat results, the four levers, the Lakhpati ring. Those are read-outs. Without a place to enter the data they're a demo, not a tool. These four frames are that place.
  • VOICE-FIRSTThe whole point is that a low-literacy user on a shared phone can keep books by speaking. The mic is the primary input on every entry surface; steppers and chips are the fallback; 🔊 reads everything back. No typed forms.
  • OFFLINEThe haat has no signal — capture (A) and close-out (B) work fully offline and sync later. The offline badge is honest about it.

Each number in #29 traces to a surface here

₹50 / जार · per-haat sale← A Log a sale (voice → confirmable card)
पिछला हाट · 18 जार · ₹900 · 4 repeat← B Haat close-out (stock + costs → profit)
इस महीने ₹6,400 · खर्च ₹2,100 · मुनाफ़ा ₹4,300← C बही-खाता ledger (running cash book)
"लागत घटाओ" & "बचत & कर्ज़" levers · Mudra instalment← D Savings & credit tracker

Primitive mapping (BetterAt platform)

  • A logged sale / expense = Evidence on a Step. "Sell at the haat" is a Step; each sale and cost is a timestamped, ₹-valued evidence entry — the same Evidence primitive nursing uses, here denominated in money.
  • B haat close-out = the Step's completion + review, producing the profit figure and repeat-customer count that feed Competency (selling) and the cockpit.
  • C ledger = the evidence log, aggregated. Month tiles and the weekly bar are roll-ups of the same entries — and they're what the CLF cohort dashboard in #30 Frame G sums across didis.
  • D savings & loan = money-capability evidence. Deposit streaks and on-time repayments are the artifacts behind "savings/credit," and the instalment is the exact item a mentor routes in #30.

Build order (after #29's read surfaces)

  • M0 — voice→structured-entry parser (qty · price · payment · product) with a confirm card; the keystone, reused by A, B and the ledger FAB.
  • M1 — Log a sale (A) · M2 — Haat close-out (B, computes profit) · M3 — बही-खाता ledger (C, the track home) · M4 — savings & Mudra tracker (D, + UPI repay).
  • Data caveat: needs a simple money-entries table (step_id, kind in/out, qty, ₹, channel cash/UPI, ts) and a savings/loan record per didi. The voice parser is the real work; the schema is small. Verify what a Jharkhand demo can seed before building.
