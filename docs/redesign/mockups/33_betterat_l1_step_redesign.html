Core move Plan → Do → Review is a lifecycle, not four equal tabs. It becomes a progress arc with one active phase; Discuss is demoted to a thread chip (it's social, orthogonal to the arc). What changed, and why it generalizes

The current screen is a 7-section form (WHAT / HOW / WHO / WHERE / BEATS / WHY / CAPABILITIES) repeated across four equal tabs, mostly empty, leaking sailing vocab into nursing. The redesign keeps every primitive but reorganizes them around the work.
1Phase arc, not four tabs. Plan → Do → Review is a lifecycle with one active phase and a progress header. Each phase shows only its own job — Plan composes, Do captures, Review confirms.
2Discuss demoted to a thread chip. It's social and orthogonal to the work lifecycle, so it lives as 💬 in the header (with count), not as a peer of the three real phases.
3Progressive disclosure on Plan. WHAT (editorial serif) + HOW lead. Who / where / why / capability collapse into one details list — killing the cold four-section scroll of empty fields.
4One timing primitive, craft-named. Shift beats = race legs = market-day beats = key holes. Same data, persona vocab — and for golf it's the hook into the strokes-gained map.
5Capture-first Do. The plan shrinks to a checklist reference; one big voice/photo/note affordance is the center of gravity. Every capture is evidence, auto-tagged to a capability.
6One capability object, three phases. The chip set in Plan accrues evidence in Do and is confirmed in Review with a real count + growth meter — replacing three disconnected capability lists.
7No vocab leaks. Every label resolves through the persona (the "see what other sailors did here" bug can't happen — the WHERE row is craft-resolved like every other).
8AI Coach inline. Moved from a standalone "Talk to AI Coach →" row into the WHAT section header (✨), where structuring help actually belongs.
