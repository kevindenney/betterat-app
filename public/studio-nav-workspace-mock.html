How this answers your questions

  • 1
    Reaching Admin: it's a surface inside the org workspace, role-gated — switch to Johns Hopkins, Admin appears. No separate hidden panel to find.
  • 2
    Three kinds of blueprints: personal-sell → Personal · Studio; org curricula → JHU · Studio; group sets → Aberdeen Fleet · Group steps. Each workspace owns its blueprints; you're never staring at one mixed pile.
  • 3
    Is a flat 3-part switcher too messy? Yes — so it's two levels: Workspace headers, surfaces nested under each. Scannable, and it scales to N orgs and groups.
  • 4
    Are interests bundled with blueprints/admin? No. Interests are a Practice-only sub-control — one level below the switcher. Keeping them out of the workspace list is what removes the mess.
  • 5
    Progressive disclosure: a plain practitioner sees only Personal · Practice. Studio appears after a first blueprint; an org block appears when you join/are-granted a role; Admin only when that role is admin.
