How to read this

The hero rows look nearly identical — at front-door scale the two blues are close, and the cream bg + Manrope do most of the "warm" work. That's the case for A: you lose almost nothing visually by unifying, and you gain one source of truth.

The feature-surface samplers are where it bites. A feature card sits on the canonical iOS gray (#F2F2F7) in SF, next to other #007AFF controls. Today #2563EB buttons land here too (193 files), so two blues collide on the same screen. Option A removes the collision; Option B only fixes it if the brighter blue is strictly fenced to auth/onboarding.

Recommendation: Option A — standardize on #007AFF, let the front-door's warmth come from the cream background + Manrope. Lower risk, matches the cutover doctrine and the marketing brand.
