Switch workspace — redesigned

The shipped sheet conflates three different things. This redesign makes the rule literal: everything in the sheet is a destination you go to — workspaces, surfaces, and interests alike. Nothing is a silent checkbox.

What's broken today

  • ✕
    Two ways to reach the same place. The Personal header goes to Practice, and there's also a "Practice" row right under it that does the same thing. Which is the real control?
  • ✕
    Interests behave differently from everything else. Tap Studio → you go to Studio. Tap Personal → you go to Personal. But tap Golf while in Studio and it only flips a checkmark underneath you — you never leave Studio. Same-looking rows, two different behaviors.
  • ✕
    An interest looks "active" on a surface where interest is meaningless. A Golf checkmark shows while you're in Studio, even though Studio isn't scoped to an interest.

The fix — one rule

An interest is the Practice destination. Tapping it means "take me to Practice, in that interest."

Practice = practicing some interest, so the interests are the Practice rows — no separate redundant "Practice" line. Tapping Golf clears any org context, switches the interest, and routes to the Practice tab. The checkmark only appears on the interest you're actually practicing right now; switch to Studio and nothing is checked, because you've left Practice.

The sheet
Workspace → its surfaces, every row a real destination
Personal's surfaces are the interests (Practice) plus Studio. Each org shows only the surfaces your role unlocks. The chevron on each row says "this navigates" — so an interest reads exactly like Studio or Admin.

What changes in ContextSheet.tsx

  • 1
    Interests become Practice destinations. switchInterestOnly → switchToInterestPractice: clear active org (setActiveOrganizationId(null)), switchInterest(slug), then router.replace('/(tabs)/practice') — mirroring switchToPersonalPractice.
  • 2
    Drop the redundant "Practice" SheetRow. The interest list is Practice. Keep a section header ("Practice — pick an interest") above the interests instead.
  • 3
    Checkmark is Practice-scoped. An interest shows its check only when !activeOrgId && surface === 'practice'. In Studio/Admin no interest is ticked — interest is meaningless there.
  • 4
    Every row gets a trailing chevron (or check when current). The affordance now matches the behavior: each row navigates. Removes the "is this a toggle or a link?" ambiguity.
  • 5
    Flatten the nesting. Drop the decorative left rail / extra indent; interests and Studio live as sibling rows inside the one Personal card, separated by a hairline. Workspace = card; surfaces = its rows.
