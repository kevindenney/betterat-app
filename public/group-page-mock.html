Group page — “4 Peaks Challenge 2027”

A peer prep group for a single race, built on the affinity-group primitive — not a club, not an org. Members are mostly RHKYC; the race is run by Aberdeen Boat Club; the group attaches to neither. It replaces the WhatsApp thread with a shared plan that converges on race day. The three dials (lifespan: event-scoped · who-pays: nobody · authority: none) put it in the lightest corner of the model — so no billing, no admin console, no vetting.

1

Affiliations are tags, not membership

“Mostly RHKYC” and “Racing with Aberdeen Boat Club” are informational context. The group isn’t owned by either org and doesn’t inherit their members, billing, or admin. A free-floating crew.

2

One dated goal anchors everything

The group points at a single event (10 Jan 2027). Same shape as a step timeline flowing toward a race anchor — prep steps are a fluid sequence; the race is the fixed point at the bottom.

3

Shared plan replaces scattered WhatsApp planning

Who’s doing what, what’s left, what’s done — assigned to people, visible to all. The part WhatsApp can’t hold. This is the reason to leave the group chat.

4

Chat is the WhatsApp replacement

Discussion lives next to the plan, not in a separate app. Banter and logistics stay; the durable decisions graduate into prep steps.

5

Invite-by-link · private & unlisted

You add people the WhatsApp way — share a link. Nothing public, so the “no vetting” worry dissolves: there’s no open-join queue to police. Join mode is invite_only.

6

No billing, no admin console, no “club”

Peer group → none of the org machinery. The word “club” never appears; this is a group (sailing voice: a one-race campaign / crew).

Same primitive, other interests

The invariant is people + shared plan + one dated goal + optional affiliation tags. Only the vocabulary and the anchor change — the chrome is identical.

Where this differs from a club/org today: a club/yacht_club/institution is verified, claimed, and authority-bearing — you join it and it can credential you. This is the opposite: you create it, you invite people, it credentials no one, and it can quietly archive after the race (or roll to “2028”). The two should never share the same creation flow or the same word. Pull study_group out of the org-kind list so a JHU student never has to “make a club” to start a study group.
