Atlas → Venue Mastery

Same tab, same data primitives — reframed around the one question a racer brings to a map: “what do I know about this water, and what will it do at race time?”
1Lenses are learning verbs, not data tables.

“Crew / Fleet / Following / My steps” become Race time · My record · Local knowledge · Fleet. Each chip changes what the map teaches, not just which dots show.

2Time scrubber pinned to the next race start.

Conditions render for Sat 14:00 (R6 start), not “now”. Drag to see the tide flip mid-race. This is Phase L’s “time-to-start scrubbing”, promoted to top chrome. Honest fallback: “No forecast yet for race time.”

3The racing area teaches, not outlines.

Favored-side shading (left, sea breeze + ebb relief), current arrows scaled by knots, a flagged tide gate at the leeward mark, start-line bias arrow. The diamond stops being dead geometry.

4Your history lives on the water.

Green dots = past races here, with your review notes pinned where they happened (“overstood layline in ebb”). Place-anchored retrospective is the missing loop: forecast → race → review → venue note.

5Venue sheet replaces the step card.

A step card on a map duplicated Practice. The sheet now answers the three venue questions — conditions at race time, your record, fleet local knowledge — and the CTA pre-stages prep for the next event (per the Atlas brief: “the map isn’t memory; it’s setup for what’s next”).

6The small group is the lens, not a filter.

Racers, village entrepreneurs, and golf groups orient around ~a dozen people. So Dragon HK is a named chip (not generic “Fleet”), the record row is you & your fleet, knowledge is attributed to named fleetmates (Mei’s 14 races > any forecast), the venue header shows 9 of 12 boats in for Saturday, and the prep CTA suggests drilling with Mei. Same primitive serves the haat network and the golf four — one group model, three dials.
